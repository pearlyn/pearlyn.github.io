Instructions

Dear participant , please read the instructions before the experiment.

There should always be an instruction before doing the actual evaluation of the interface.

In this experiment, we are trying to compare between 2 techniques Traditional copy-paste technique and AutoComPaste.

Definition

Traditional copy-paste is a technique where participant will use (Ctrl-C + Ctrl-V) to copy and paste a text that is of interest from one document to another.

AutoComPaste is a technique where participant will use keyword search for a text that is of interest and the program will populate the entire text based on the keyword in a document.

How to use AutoComPaste?

For example, you are required to copy the text of interest as follows “Power attained in an AC circuit as a product of effective voltage and current which reach their peak at different times.” to a designated document. If you type the first word, “Power” at the document, you will find a search results that matched the word “Power”.

To narrow down the search results, type more keywords. There is only 1 search result that matched the keyword “Power atta”. Hit enter to view the entire text.

Verify that it is the sentence you are tasked to copy to the document.

Procedures

Before the experiment, you will be asked to answer a series of simple questions about yourself. Thereafter, you will proceed with the tasks.

There will be a total of 24 tasks to complete which requires the participant to use either Traditional copy-paste technique or AutoComPaste to complete the tasks based on the instruction given at the left panel (see green box below). The text to copy is found in the red text area below.

Once you have completed a task, click on next task to move to the next task (see purple box below).

After you have completed all the tasks, you will be asked to answer a series of post-experiment questions to understand better on how you feel about Traditional copy-paste technique and AutoComPaste technique.
